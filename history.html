Blaise Pascal's Brief History

Important History of Blaise Pascal

Born: June 19, 1623, Clermont-Ferrand, France

Died: August 19, 1662, Paris aged 39

Blaise Pascal helped his father, Étienne Pascal, primarily due to family circumstances and his father's health. Étienne was a tax collector in Rouen, and as he faced difficulties with his work and health issues, Blaise took on responsibilities to assist him. This included engaging in mathematical work and developing inventions, which not only supported his father's career but also allowed Blaise to pursue his own intellectual interests. Their close relationship and collaboration fostered an environment that encouraged Blaise's later groundbreaking contributions to mathematics and science.

In 1642, he started some pioneering work on calculating machines (called Pascal's calculators and later Pascalines), establishing him as one of the first two inventors of the mechanical calculator
